QUICK FUN FACTS

  • Marmots are related to squirrels we see in our backyards. In fact, they are classified as ground squirrels. Yes, that makes a groundhog a marmot and by definition a ground squirrel.
  • Marmots' diet consists mostly of grass and other vegetation, which isn't very nutritious. That makes them vegetarian (and very hungry ones at that).
  • Marmots primarily live in the Northern Hemisphere—US, Canada, Mexico—but can also be found in Central Europe and Asia.

Habitat and Behavior

Marmots typically inhabit mountainous areas and prefer open grassy regions where they can burrow. They are known for their social behavior and often live in colonies.

Conservation Status

While some marmot species are abundant, others face threats due to habitat loss and climate change. Conservation efforts are underway to protect their natural habitats and ensure their survival.

Interesting Tidbits

Marmots are known for their whistle-like calls, which they use to communicate with each other. During summer, they are active and can often be seen sunbathing or foraging for food.

Marmots at a National Park

Olympic Marmots live at Olympic National Park.
